About the Program

What Are Advocacy Bootcamps?

The DLT Advocacy Bootcamp is an intensive training program designed to transform people with Parkinson's — and their care partners — into skilled, confident self-advocates.

You'll learn how to navigate the healthcare system, communicate effectively with your medical team, access resources, understand your rights, and advocate for policy change at the community and national level.

Bootcamps are led by Bryce Perry, who has spent years studying and practicing advocacy in the Parkinson's space. The curriculum is constantly updated to reflect the current healthcare landscape.

📣

Who Should Attend?

  • ✓People newly diagnosed with Parkinson's
  • ✓Long-term Parkinson's patients ready to take charge
  • ✓Care partners and family members
  • ✓Patient advocates and community leaders
  • ✓Healthcare professionals who want to understand the patient experience
  • ✓Anyone who wants to turn their diagnosis into a platform for change
What You'll Learn

Curriculum Overview

Six comprehensive modules covering every aspect of Parkinson's advocacy.

1

Understanding Your Diagnosis

Demystify Parkinson's — what it is, what it isn't, and what it means for your life. Learn the language your doctors use and what questions to ask.

2

Navigating the Healthcare System

Learn how to find specialists, prepare for appointments, get second opinions, and ensure your voice is heard in every medical encounter.

3

Building Your Care Team

Who should be on your team? How do you build relationships with providers? What is a movement disorder specialist and how do you get one?

4

Insurance, Access & Resources

Navigate insurance challenges, access financial assistance, find clinical trials, and leverage every resource available to the Parkinson's community.

5

Communicating Your Story

Learn how to tell your story effectively — to providers, family members, employers, and the public. Your story is your most powerful advocacy tool.

6

Community & Policy Advocacy

From local community organizing to Capitol Hill advocacy days — learn how to turn your personal experience into systemic change.

Your Instructor

Meet Bryce Perry

Bryce Perry is a Parkinson's advocate who has spent years studying the art and science of patient self-advocacy. After his own diagnosis, he navigated the healthcare system, fought for access to the best care, and turned those hard-won lessons into a curriculum that has transformed hundreds of lives.

He brings warmth, humor, and hard-won experience to every session. This isn't a lecture — it's a conversation between people who have been there and know what it takes to advocate effectively.

Bryce also speaks nationally on advocacy topics. Learn more at bryceperry.org.
