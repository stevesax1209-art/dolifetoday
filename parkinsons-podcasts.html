Podcasts About Parkinson's

Conversations, stories, and real life with Parkinson's — two weekly podcasts from people who live with the disease, hosted by Bryce Perry.

Show #1

Living with Parkinson's: The Good, the Bad, and the Reality

Hosted by Parkinson's advocate Bryce Perry, this podcast explores the real-life experiences of living with Parkinson's. Episodes blend personal stories, practical advice, and conversations about the challenges and victories that come with the condition.

Topics often include navigating symptoms, medication strategies, mindset and resilience, daily routines and lifestyle, and personal reflections from Bryce's own journey.

Show #2

Life in Motion: The Parkinson's Podcast

Life in Motion is a podcast hosted by Bryce Perry and Brian Campkin. Both hosts live with Parkinson's themselves, bringing humor, honesty, and community connection to conversations about the disease.

Episodes include discussions about daily life with Parkinson's, community stories, and insights about navigating the condition. The show blends information with humor and shared experience.

Why Podcasts Help

Why Podcasts Matter for People with Parkinson's

Living with Parkinson's often raises many questions. Podcasts allow people to hear discussions about treatment options, lifestyle changes, emotional challenges, exercise and wellness, and research and medical updates.

Hearing real conversations can help people feel informed, supported, and less alone. Many of our listeners say the podcast was their first step toward finding the Doing Life Today community.

📻

Topics Covered

  • 🌿Lifestyle changes and daily routines
  • 😊Emotional challenges and mental health
  • 🏃Exercise and wellness strategies
  • 🔬Research updates and clinical trials
  • 🤝Caregiving and family dynamics
About Doing Life Today

Real Talk. Real Life. Real Community.

Doing Life Today is a platform created by Parkinson's advocate Bryce Perry. After being diagnosed with Parkinson's at age 40, Bryce began sharing his experiences publicly to help others better understand the disease and navigate life with it.

Today the platform includes podcasts, videos, programs, and a growing community for people navigating Parkinson's.
