Our Story

We Started with a Diagnosis
and a Microphone

The story of how one conversation became a community of 100,000+ people navigating Parkinson's together.

The Beginning

A Diagnosis. A Decision. A Community.

When Bryce Perry received his Parkinson's diagnosis, he did what most people do: he went home, opened his laptop, and searched for answers. What he found was medical information — but not community. Not real voices. Not people talking honestly about what it actually felt like to live with this disease.

So he decided to create what he couldn't find. With a basic microphone and no podcast experience, Bryce hit record. He talked about the fear. The uncertainty. The weird dark humor that can emerge when you're facing something this big. He posted it online, expecting maybe a dozen listeners.

Thousands showed up.

That first episode was the seed of what would become Doing Life Today — a place where the Parkinson's community could find not just information, but connection, advocacy, and hope rooted in honesty.

What Drives Us

Our Mission & Values

Everything we build and everything we do flows from these core commitments.

💬

Radical Honesty

We don't pretend Parkinson's is easy. We don't minimize the hard parts. We create space for the full reality of this journey — the grief, the humor, the breakthroughs, and everything in between.

🤝

Peer Power

We believe lived experience is a form of expertise. The wisdom of our community members is at the heart of everything we do. No one understands Parkinson's better than people living with it.

🔬

Evidence-Informed

We partner with medical professionals and researchers to ensure our content is grounded in current science — while never losing our human voice.

♿

Accessibility First

Our platforms are designed with accessibility at the core. Large text, high contrast, screen reader compatibility, closed captions — every tool built for every member.

📣

Advocacy

We equip our community to advocate — for themselves in medical settings, for policy change, and for the broader Parkinson's cause in their communities.

🌱

Growth Mindset

A diagnosis is not a destination. We believe in continual growth — physically, emotionally, and spiritually — and we build tools that support that growth every day.

Meet Your Host

Bryce Perry

Bryce Perry is a Parkinson's advocate, speaker, and community builder who has dedicated his post-diagnosis life to transforming how the Parkinson's community connects, accesses resources, and advocates for itself.

A former [professional background], Bryce brought his skills as a communicator and community organizer to the Parkinson's world with one goal: to make sure no one ever has to face this diagnosis alone.

He speaks nationally on topics including self-advocacy, community building, and the intersection of technology and patient empowerment. His work has been recognized by major Parkinson's organizations and featured in national media.

Outside of DLT, Bryce maintains an active speaking and consulting practice through bryceperry.org, where organizations can learn about booking him for conferences, events, and corporate wellness programs.
